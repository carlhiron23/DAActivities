# Safeguarding Policy

D A Activities is committed to protecting the welfare and safety of all participants, staff, volunteers and visitors. This safeguarding policy sets out our approach to preventing abuse and promoting safe, respectful environments for older adults and other vulnerable people we support.

## Purpose

This policy aims to: 
- Ensure everyone involved with D A Activities understands their responsibilities for safeguarding.
- Provide clear reporting pathways for concerns or allegations of abuse.
- Promote safe working practices and reduce risk.

## Scope

Applies to all staff, volunteers, contractors and trustees working on behalf of D A Activities across all locations and activities.

## Principles

- The welfare of the person is paramount.
- Everyone has the right to live free from abuse and neglect.
- All concerns will be taken seriously and responded to promptly and appropriately.

## Responsibilities

- Leadership: ensure training, safe recruitment and clear reporting procedures are in place.
- Staff & volunteers: follow the policy, attend training, report concerns immediately.
- Designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL): receive reports, coordinate responses and liaise with statutory agencies.

## Reporting a Concern

1. If someone is in immediate danger, call the emergency services.
2. Report concerns to the DSL as soon as possible (within 24 hours). Provide factual information — who, what, when, where.
3. The DSL will assess risk, record the concern, and, where appropriate, refer to statutory services (social care, police) following local safeguarding procedures.

## Confidentiality

Information will be shared on a need-to-know basis. Records will be kept securely and retained in line with data protection rules and organisational retention schedules.

## Safe Recruitment & Training

We will undertake appropriate background checks where roles require them, provide safeguarding training to staff and volunteers, and maintain clear role descriptions and supervision.

## Allegations Against Staff or Volunteers

Allegations will be handled promptly and fairly. The DSL will follow local authority guidance for managing allegations and may suspend individuals temporarily while an investigation is carried out.

## Review

This policy will be reviewed annually or after any significant safeguarding incident.

Contact: For any safeguarding concerns, contact the Designated Safeguarding Lead at D A Activities (email or phone as provided internally).
